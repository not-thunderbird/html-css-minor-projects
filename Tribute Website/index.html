Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man of India

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam earned the title "Missile Man of India" for his outstanding contributions to India’s missile development and defense technologies. As a leading scientist at DRDO and ISRO, he was the mastermind behind the successful development of Agni and Prithvi missiles, which placed India among the top nations in defense technology. His leadership during the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998 showcased India’s scientific strength to the world. But beyond his scientific genius, Dr. Kalam was a man of simplicity, integrity, and great wisdom. He always believed in the power of dreams, innovation, and education. His speeches, books, and interactions with students continue to ignite young minds across the country. Even after becoming the 11th President of India, he remained connected to the youth, encouraging them to become nation-builders and to never give up on their goals.

Biography

  • Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, to a humble boat-owner’s family.
  • Graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and later studied aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT).
  • Joined ISRO in 1969 and played a key role in India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III) that deployed the Rohini satellite in 1980.
  • Known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on ballistic missile and space launch vehicle technology at DRDO and ISRO.
  • Served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007, becoming one of the most loved and respected Presidents.
  • Authored several inspiring books like *Wings of Fire*, *Ignited Minds*, and *India 2020*, encouraging youth to dream big and work for the nation.
